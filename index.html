Abstract

Voice conversion (VC) is confronted with the trade-off between speaker features disentanglement and speech quality. To address this challenge, we introduce MinFlow-VC, a voice conversion (VC) framework based on conditional flow matching, which is further enhanced via min-pooling adversarial training to improve the con version system. We adopt a KNN-based approach to directly convert the source speaker features into a shared speaker space that effec tively removes the source speaker timbre information, which serves as the content features. To further improve the generation quality, we propose a min-pooling adversarial training method that not only automatically detects and corrects inconsistencies caused by KNN but also further improves the generation quality of conditional flow matching. Experimental results demonstrate that our method outper formsotherstrongVCbaselinesintermsofnaturalness, intelligibility and timbre similarity.
